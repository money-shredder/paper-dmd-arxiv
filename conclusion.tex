\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper introduces $\mathrm{D}^3$-Cache,
a training-free acceleration method
for diffusion inference
that leverages Dynamic Mode Decomposition.
By fitting a low-rank linear model
to short-window transformer-output dynamics
and gating cached reuse with measured relative-\( \ell_2 \) preview error,
$\mathrm{D}^3$-Cache predicts denoising updates
and skips redundant forward passes.
Across text-to-image (FLUX.1-dev, HiDream-I1-full)
and text-to-video (Wan2.1) models,
$\mathrm{D}^3$-Cache delivers consistent over 2.5$\times$ speedups
while preserving perceptual quality,
outperforming TeaCache, MagCache, and TaylorSeer
in speed-quality trade-offs
with a compact memory footprint.

\section*{Impact Statement}

This paper presents work whose goal
is to advance the efficiency of diffusion model inference.
There are many potential positive societal consequences of our work,
and we don't see any direct negative impacts
that must be specifically highlighted here.
